𝘼𝙃 𝙃𝙊𝙇𝙔 𝙅𝙀𝙎𝙐𝙎 𝙃𝙊𝙒 𝙃𝘼𝙎𝙏 𝙏𝙃𝙊𝙐 𝙊𝙁𝙁𝙀𝙉𝘿𝙀𝘿 
Composer: Johann Heermann (1630)
Translation: Robert Bridges (1899)

1. Ah, holy Jesus, 
   how hast Thou offended,
   That man to judge Thee
   hath in hate pretended?
   By foes derided,
   by Thine own rejected,
   O most afflicted!

2. Who was the guilty?
   Who brought this upon Thee?
   Alas, my treason, Jesus,
   hath undone Thee!
   'Twas I, Lord Jesus, 
   I it was denied Thee;
   I crucified Thee.

3. Lo, the Good Shepherd
   for the sheep is offered;
   The slave hath sinned,
   and the Son hath suffered.
   For man's transgression
   His own Lord chastiseth,
   God abandoneth.

4. For me, kind Jesus,
   was Thy incarnation,
   Thy mortal sorrow, 
   and Thy life's oblation;
   Thy death of anguish
   and Thy bitter passion,
   For my salvation.

5. Therefore, kind Jesus,
   since I cannot pay Thee,
   I do adore Thee, 
   and will ever pray Thee,
   Think on Thy pity 
   and Thy love unswerving,
   Not my deserving.
